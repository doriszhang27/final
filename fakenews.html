FAKE NEWS & DISINFORMATION

What is fake news?

Fake news is a term that refers to false or misleading information that is spread intentionally or unintentionally through various media platforms, such as social media, websites, newspapers, television, etc. Fake news can have different causes and motivations, such as political propaganda, ideological bias, financial gain, personal agenda, or simply ignorance or error.

What is disinformation?

Disinformation is false information deliberately spread to deceive people. Disinformation is an orchestrated adversarial activity in which actors insert strategic deceptions and media manipulation tactics to advance political, military, or commercial goals.

Relationship between fake news and disinformation

"Fake news" has sometimes been categorized as a type of disinformation, but scholars have advised not using these two terms interchangeably or using "fake news" altogether in academic writing since politicians have weaponized it to describe any unfavorable news coverage or information.

Why is fake news and disinformation a problem?

Fake news and disinformation can have negative impacts on both the public and the individual level.

  • On the public level, they can influence public opinion, undermine trust in institutions, incite violence, and harm democracy.
  • On the individual level, they can affect one's beliefs, attitudes, emotions, and behaviors. For example, fake news can cause fear, anger, anxiety, or confusion, and can lead to poor decision making.

How can we identify and combat fake news and disinformation?

There are some tips and strategies that can help us identify and combat them, such as:

  • Check the source of the information. Is it credible, reputable, and transparent? Does it provide evidence and references for its claims?

  • Check the content of the information. Is it consistent, logical, and factual? Does it match with other reliable sources?

  • Check the context of the information. Is it relevant, timely, and accurate? Does it consider different perspectives and opinions?

What are some examples of fake news and disinformation?

The claim that COVID-19 was created in a lab in China.

The claim that the 2020 US presidential election was rigged by voter fraud.

The claim that drinking bleach can cure cancer.
